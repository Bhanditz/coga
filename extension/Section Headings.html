Section Headings

Section headings are provided that organize the content.

Suggestion for Priority Level: AAA

Related Glossary additions or changes

none

Principle and Guideline

Principle 3, Guideline 3.1

Description

Section headings and subheadings visually represent how information is organized and succinctly tells the reader what is in each section.

Benefits

Headings and especially subheadings are a roadmap for readers: they know what to expect and where to find it. This kind of grouping of information (or "chunking") is helpful to guide the reader. Readers should be able to skim headings and subheadings to get an idea of the layout of the content.

Related Resources (optional)

none

Testability

Procedure

  1. Check that headings exist
  2. Headings and subheadings are used to visually convey importance of the content topics

Expected Results

  • All checks above are true

Techniques

  1. Making headings available using personalizations. With personalization headings can be invisible or hidden by the user.
  2. Making headings visually convey importance by subheadings being smaller font than preceding headings.
  3. Note: Clear headings are covered by 2.4.6
