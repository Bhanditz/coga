SC Shortname: Safety

SC Text

Do not automatically choose options that may disadvantage users without their approval, or add mechanisms that are likely to confuse users in a way that may do them harm.

Note that this does not include selling products know to be harmful.

Suggestion for Priority Level

Priority Level A

Related Glossary additions or changes

None

What Principle and Guideline the SC falls within.

Principle 2: Operable.

Current:

Guideline 2.3 "Do not design content in a way that is known to cause seizures."

Proposed:

Guideline 2.3 "Do not create mechanisms that are known to potentially harm the user."

Exclusion: Mechanisms known to potentially harm do not include selling products known to be harmful.

Description

This success criteria prevents people from tricking users, in a way that specifically targets people with cognitive disabilities and claiming they are conformance to specifications for accessibility and inclusion.

For example, some ecommerce sites automatically incrementing the number of items selected without warning the user. Where as most users will notice that they have ordered two bikes, and that the price is double the anticipated price. Most people would notice the change in the final price, however someone with a disability such as dementia or severe dyscalculia may be unable to notice the change.

Having been subject to these tricks users lose confidence in their ability to perform tasks online. The cumulative effects of this type of behavior is many users who need access to ecommerce will be the least likely to be able to use it.

 

Benefits

The benefit of this success criteria is that users can be safe online , and that sites that conform to this specification are safe. The need of this success criteria is more fully discussed at Online Safety Issue Paper and user needs table 1: Authentication and Safety

Testability

Procedure

  1. Are there any automatic choices or decisions made for the user such as option being selections.
  2. If there are options from step 1 confirm that they can not do the user harm
  3. Confirm that no other failure techniques occur

 

Techniques

Techniques

  • Are there no automatics choices or decisions made for the user
  • Are there automatics choices or decisions made for the user, but they could not harm the user
  • Where leaving the site or task is likely to cause harm, ensure that the user is aware of the change and risks. For example, in a health care site when looking for one drug, and an advert takes the user to by a different drug
  • Users are regularly warned to avoid scams.

Failure techniques

  • Adding automatically incrementing the number of items selected without warning the use .

  • automatically choose options that may disadvantage the user without their approval.
  • adding mechanisms likely to confuse the user in a way that may do them harm.
