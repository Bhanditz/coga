SC Shortname: Safety

SC Text

Do not add mechanisms that are likely to confuse the user in a way that may do them harm.

Suggestion for Priority Level

Priority Level A

Related Glossary additions or changes

None

What Principle and Guideline the SC falls within.

Principle 2: Operable.

Current:

Guideline 2.3 "Do not design content in a way that is known to cause seizures."

Proposed:

Guideline 2.3 "Do not create mechanisms that are known to potentially harm the user."

Exclusion: Mechanisms known to potentially harm do not include selling products known to be harmful.

Description

Safety should be priority when making content accessibile for people with cognitive disabilities. Extra care should be applied at the same time to keep them safe. All user information must be kept safe, to the fullest extent possible.

Benefits

Being able to engage in transactions safely and securily benefits all users. Those with cognitive disabilities will particulary benefit from transaction systems designed so that any information implying vulnerabilities is on the user device and is secure.

Testability

Procedure

  1. Use known techniques to keep user information safe, such as keeping it on the user's device.
  2. Warn the user of any known risk.
  3. Do not automatically choose options that may disadvantage the user without their approval.
  4. Do not add mechanisms likely to confuse the user in a way that may do them harm.
  5. Ensure that clues the user has cognitive disabilities, such as a request for a simplified version, is protected information.
  6. Ensure that security is easily implemented by the user, such as with a biometric device.

Techniques

  • Chat options prevent any exchange of personal information.
  • Users are regularly warned to avoid scams.
  • Getting help and or reporting something worrying is easy-to-do, and includes a statement users will never be penalized for reporting something.
  • An easy-to-use feature is provided for users to report to the cyber crime fighters in their jurisdiction.
  • Easy-to-use videos and tips with explanations are provided about cyber criminals, how to stay safe, and how to report anything found odd.
  • Server-side solutions, such as analytics, are employed to find cyber-criminals.
  • Advertisements and paid articles are clearly marked, as external content, in an easy-to-understand way.
  • Users are informed when they are exiting a web site, including via links from other users.
  • Sites offering sexual content, or intended for chats of sexual nature, state that clearly.
